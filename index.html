Potato Bonanza Extravaganza

Potato Bonanza Extravaganza is an 11-week game prototype, made in Unity, initially conceived as a humorous adventure featuring a secret-agent potato tasked with freeing fellow vegetables from captivity and ultimately facing off against an evil head chef. The final iteration evolved into an action-packed experience where players control a heroic potato agent fighting against waves of animated kitchen utensils and cookware. Throughout development, the project emphasised strong practices in version control, quality assurance, and collaborative coding contributions. I personally implemented critical gameplay systems such as power-ups, shooting mechanics, and player input controls, and actively participated in quality assurance processes. The project was nominated as one of the Best Group Game Projects at the University of Greenwich SharkExpo 2023/24, highlighting the team's dedication to creativity, effective teamwork, and technical excellence.

Reign of Elements - Legacy Of The Dragon Mages

Reign of Elements - Legacy of the Dragon Mages is a compelling 2-week game prototype, made in Unity, designed to immerse players in an intriguing narrative set across multiple atmospheric dungeons. The game combines rich storytelling with engaging gameplay to deliver a memorable player experience. During development, I focused on showcasing Object-Oriented Programming principles by creating customised sprites, coding dynamic AI behaviours, and implementing responsive character controls. Additionally, I integrated puzzle mechanics and fundamental combat features to enhance gameplay depth and player engagement. This project was recognised for its creativity and technical execution, earning a nomination as one of the Best Individual Game Projects at the University of Greenwich SharkExpo 2023/24.

The Bizarre Gramophone

The Bizarre Gramophone is an innovative 5-week Arduino puzzle box prototype developed as a group project under the theme "Bizarre Arcade," showcasing a distinctive steampunk-inspired gramophone design. This unique project challenges and intrigues players with thoughtfully designed puzzles and interactive elements, blending mechanical creativity with engaging electronic interactions. Throughout the development process, I contributed extensively to puzzle box design, carefully planning and implementing complex puzzle mechanics. My primary responsibility was designing and assembling robust electrical circuits, while I also collaborated closely with a team member on programming the interactive features using C++. The project exemplifies a harmonious integration of steampunk aesthetics, mechanical design, electronics, and software programming, creating an immersive and unforgettable puzzle-solving experience.
